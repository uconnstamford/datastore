Addictive Algorithms

The pervasive use of algorithm-driven content on social media platforms has raised significant concerns regarding its impact on children's mental health and well-being. Find resources below to learn more about addictive algorithms and what's at stake.

The Problem with Addictive Algorithms

What's Happening?

Social media companies deploy powerful algorithms designed to capture attention and maximize user engagement. These algorithms adapt to user behavior, often promoting content that is emotionally triggering or sensational, regardless of its effect on young audiences.

Why It Matters for Children:

  • Mental Health Risks: Children who spend more than three hours a day on social platforms are twice as likely to experience anxiety or depression.
  • Addictive Interfaces: Infinite scrolling, autoplay, and tailored push notifications encourage compulsive usage.
  • Unsafe Recommendations: Algorithms sometimes push harmful content like self-harm videos or eating disorder tips.
  • Diminished Control: Kids often don't realize they are being targeted and manipulated for engagement.

Legislation Spotlight — The Kids Online Safety Act

What is KOSA?

The Kids Online Safety Act (KOSA), championed by Senator Richard Blumenthal, is a bipartisan legislative effort designed to:

  • Disable addictive content features for minors by default.
  • Require clear opt-outs from algorithmic targeting.
  • Give parents more control over content filters and time limits.
  • Increase transparency around how platforms recommend content to underage users.

This act is a step toward enforcing ethical responsibility in tech design and prioritizing children's safety over corporate profits.
